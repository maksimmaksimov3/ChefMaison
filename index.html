The opportunity

People decide what to do long before they search for a chef.

More and more of those early decisions now start with an AI assistant, not Google. The brands that get named inside the AI's answer win the customer before a single competitor is compared. Today, at that awareness moment, the private-chef category is invisible.

Memory answers

ChatGPT and Gemini answering from training data. Hard to shift, and only over the long term with presence everywhere. Not where one blog wins.

Retrieval answers

Perplexity, ChatGPT search and Google AI Overviews pull live web pages and cite them. A single well-built page can get cited here in weeks. This is our target.

The wire we build

Connect the occasion ("a birthday in Amsterdam") to the answer ("a private chef at home"). The blog is that wire, written to be quoted by the retrieval engines.

Present where we already compete

Why birthday, Amsterdam, English

The topic wasn't a guess. The data chose it.

Birthday: the cleanest gap

Of all occasions, birthday has the most consistent search demand and the clearest empty slot. Google's AI Overview for birthday ideas lists boats and bars, never a chef.

Amsterdam: home advantage

ChefMaison's strongest market and the city where it already wins the cost answer. We extend an existing strength rather than build authority from zero.

English: the right audience

The data split cleanly: English occasion queries come from internationals and visitors, exactly the audience weighing "what should I do" with no private-chef answer in sight.

The bridge already exists

Because Google already cites ChefMaison for cost, one occasion-led blog can pull that authority forward into the awareness moment. Short path, real upside.

The measurement plan

We re-run the exact same three prompts across the same three engines 2 to 4 weeks after Post 1 is published, logged side by side with today's baseline. If the gap starts closing on the retrieval engines, we scale the format to more occasions and cities. If not, we have lost nothing but learned exactly where the resistance is.

"The category isn't invisible. It's just not connected to the occasion yet. That's the one wire we're building."

Next: publish

Owners validate the guest-story details, confirm robots.txt allows AI crawlers, then publish with schema.

Then: measure

Re-run the baseline at the 2–4 week mark and compare. One page, fast read on whether AEO moves.

If it works: scale

Repeat for anniversaries, dinner parties, and new cities. A Dutch-language post extends the cost win locally.
